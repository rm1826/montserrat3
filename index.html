MONTSERRAT HISTORY

Inspired by the signage and typography of the Montserrat neighborhood in Buenos Aires, reflecting the urban landscape and architectural style.

She gathered inspiration from the 1920-1950s lettering on street signs, posters, painted windows, and cafe canopies in the homonymous neighborhood. During the course of her studies, she realized that these letterforms were not as common as they once had been, so she felt compelled to "rescue the beauty of urban typography" through a new font under a free and open source license.

Montserrat has gained popularity as a free alternative to other similar sans-serif fonts, such as Gotham or Avenir. Gaumont Cinema near the Congressional Plaza helped inspire the creation of the Montserrat typeface.
